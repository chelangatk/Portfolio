About Me

PhD Candidate | Researcher | Change-Maker

Biography

I am a final-year PhD candidate in Electrical Engineering at the University of Texas at Dallas, specializing in the sustainability of electric propulsion systems and electrified transportation. My research focuses on lifecycle analysis approaches to optimize energy consumption and enhance the performance of energy-efficient electric vehicles.

With over 5 years of experience in renewable energy systems, I've led strategic consulting, research, and global innovation initiatives across Africa and the United States. My work spans power systems analysis, microgrid development, electric vehicle integration, and sustainable energy solutions that drive real-world impact.

As a Graduate Research Intern at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (Summer 2025), I contributed to the design and optimization of rare-earth-free electric motor technologies for medium and heavy-duty electric vehicles, supporting DOE initiatives in sustainable transportation and critical materials resilience. I have also served as Business and Fund Development Director for IEEE Smart Village, where I secured over $100K in funding for solar microgrid projects impacting healthcare, agriculture, and tourism sectors across East Africa.

I am deeply committed to using engineering and innovation to address climate change, advance electrification, and create sustainable solutions that empower communities globally.
